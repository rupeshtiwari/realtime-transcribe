CAMPERSO Candidate Learning Path - Focused Book Roadmap

-------------------------------------------------------------
Fundamentals of Data Engineering (Joe Reis, Matt Housley)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Pipelines & Orchestration: lifecycle of pipelines, modern orchestration tools (Airflow, Dagster).
- Storage & Compute: OLTP vs OLAP, lakehouse tradeoffs.
- Serving Data: BI, ML, latency considerations.
Why It Helps: Builds the practical vocabulary and mental model to explain end-to-end tradeoffs in interviews.

-------------------------------------------------------------
Designing Data-Intensive Applications (Martin Kleppmann)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Data Models & Query Languages (Ch. 2): relational vs document vs graph.
- Storage & Retrieval (Ch. 3): indexing (B-Trees, LSM-trees).
- Encoding & Evolution (Ch. 4): schema evolution with Avro/Protobuf.
- Batch Processing (Ch. 10) + Stream Processing (Ch. 11): frameworks, guarantees, idempotency.
Why It Helps: The theory bible. Gives you tradeoff language (SQL vs NoSQL, batch vs streaming) FAANG expects.

-------------------------------------------------------------
Streaming Systems (Tyler Akidau)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Windowing & Time Semantics: event time vs processing time, watermarks.
- Exactly-Once Processing: checkpoints, idempotent sinks.
- Stateful Streaming: scaling Spark/Flink jobs.
Why It Helps: Prepares you for late data, ordering, retriesâ€”core in stream processing interviews.

-------------------------------------------------------------
The Data Warehouse Toolkit (Ralph Kimball)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Dimensional Modeling: fact vs dimension, grain, surrogate keys.
- Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCD): handling history with SCD2.
- Fact Table Types: transactional, snapshot, accumulating snapshot.
Why It Helps: Covers data modeling rounds. Teaches how to design BI schemas for analytics.

-------------------------------------------------------------
Star Schema: The Complete Reference (Christopher Adamson)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Core Star Schema Patterns: denormalization, joins, star vs snowflake.
- Common Pitfalls: schema mistakes to avoid.
Why It Helps: Reinforces Kimball and sharpens schema design skills for SQL rounds.

-------------------------------------------------------------
Software Architecture: The Hard Parts (Neal Ford, Mark Richards)
-------------------------------------------------------------
What to Read:
- Architectural Trade-offs: consistency vs availability, coupling vs decoupling.
- Event-Driven Systems: async communication, microservices.
Why It Helps: Helps you defend design tradeoffs at senior interviews (Kafka vs API, Redis vs Memcached).
